Information about La Liga

  • La Liga, officially known as La Liga Santander for sponsorship reasons, is the top professional football league in Spain. It is one of the most popular sports leagues in the world, with an average of 2.8 billion people watching each season. La Liga is also the most profitable football league in the world, generating €3.9 billion in revenue in the 2019–20 season.
  • The league consists of 20 teams that compete for the La Liga title, commonly referred to as La Liga Santander. The season runs from August to May, with each team playing each other twice, once at home and once away. The top three teams at the end of the season qualify for the UEFA Champions League, while the fourth-placed team enters the UEFA Europa League. The bottom three teams are relegated to Segunda División.
  • La Liga is renowned for its fast-paced and attacking style of play. The league has produced some of the greatest players in the history of the sport, including Lionel Messi, Cristiano Ronaldo, Zinedine Zidane, and Diego Maradona.
  • La Liga is admired for its high-quality competition and technically skilled players. Teams like Real Madrid, FC Barcelona, Atlético Madrid, and Sevilla FC consistently demonstrate their prowess, enticing crowds and spectators worldwide. The league's rich history and tradition, dating back to the late 19th century, add to its allure. La Liga is a stage where players reach their full potential and captivate audiences with their unparalleled talents.
  • La Liga's popularity stems from its ability to provide an exhilarating and unpredictable spectacle. The league's competitive nature and the presence of world-class players ensure that every match is a thrilling event. La Liga's global reach has made it a cultural phenomenon, attracting fans from all corners of the globe.
  • La Liga's best teams and players have consistently captivated audiences worldwide. Real Madrid, FC Barcelona, and Atlético Madrid have dominated the league for decades, showcasing their exceptional skills and determination. Players like Lionel Messi, Karim Benzema, Vinicius Jr., Mohamed Salah, and Vinícius Júnior have etched their names in La Liga's history, mesmerizing fans with their brilliance.
